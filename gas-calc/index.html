⚠️ CRITICAL DISCLAIMERS & LIABILITY
  • Local Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) Approval Required: All installations must comply with local building codes, natural gas / LP safety regulations, and AHJ inspection. This calculator is a pre-design aid only and does not replace professional engineering or permit requirements.
  • Generac Specification Compliance: Generator inlet pressures are verified against Generac spec sheets (Guardian 3.5–7" WC NG | 10–12" WC LP; Protector 5–14" WC NG/LP). Field measurement required—this tool assumes ideal conditions.
  • NFPA 54/58 & ANSI Z21.75 Compliance: Pipe sizing follows NFPA 54 §402.4 Spitzglass formula. All flex connectors must meet ANSI Z21.75 or equivalent (recommend 14" stainless steel connectors at meter and generator for safety). Black steel, PE, CSST, and TracPipe all supported; verify local approvals.
  • Pressure Drop Verification: This tool assumes ~10% pressure loss per stage over 100 ft typical piping. Real-world drops depend on fittings, bends, valve placement, and simultaneous load. Measure final inlet pressure with manometer before generator commissioning.
  • Altitude Derate Assumptions: Guardian derating starts at 500 ft (−3.5% per 1,000 ft); Protector at 0 ft (−3% per 1,000 ft). Confirm with Generac for high-altitude or industrial applications.
  • BTU Normalization & Actual Fuel Content: Base BTU values use standardized conversions (NG: 1,020 BTU/ft³ @ STP; LP: 2,516 BTU/ft³ @ STP, SG = 1.52). Actual content varies by elevation, temperature, humidity, and fuel composition. Professional load analysis recommended for critical standby systems.
  • Load Calculator Reserve Margin: 20% default reserve is recommended for standby duty; adjust per site requirements. Final generator must be sized at 80% of calculated load minimum (NFPA 110).
  • No Liability for Errors or Omissions: Always On Generators and this calculator assume no liability for pipe oversizing, undersizing, installation errors, code violations, property damage, injury, or system failure. Professional design, installation, and inspection by licensed contractors required.

  1. Download/Print Results: Export this calculation (PDF recommended) for AHJ submittal.
  2. Licensed Installer: Contract a licensed HVAC/gas contractor for design verification and installation.
  3. Pressure Verification: Use field manometer to confirm meter pressure, service regulator outlet, and final generator inlet pressure.
